# 27 — Naming and Substrate Policy

## Naming

Use:

- Chiral Narrative Synthesis
- CNS
- CNS 8.0

Do not rename the project around a grounding subsystem.

## Substrate language

Use:

- grounding substrate;
- proof substrate;
- access-aware substrate;
- possible-world uncertainty layer.

Avoid naming the substrate as if it were the theory.

## Direct architectural wording

Preferred:

> CNS 8.0 constrains synthesized SNOs with evidence atoms, access states, tensor proof traces, possible-world support, and oracle-boundary checks.

Preferred:

> The synthesis engine operates over chiral, evidentially entangled SNOs.

Avoid:

> shift from narrative synthesis to evidence-first ranking.

Avoid:

> framework for likely truth ranking.

Avoid:

> evidence-first system with narrative output.

## Rule

If a sentence makes ranking, access, or audit sound like the main mechanism, rewrite it around SNO synthesis.

## Public title pattern

Use:

```text
Chiral Narrative Synthesis 8.0: Grounded Dialectical Orthesis
```

or:

```text
CNS 8.0: Proof-Carrying Narrative Synthesis under Chiral Tension and Limited Information
```
