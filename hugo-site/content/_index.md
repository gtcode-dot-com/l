## Our Mission

### Public-Interest Records Audit for Hawaii

Oahu Underground exists to document accountability gaps in Hawaii through records-first reporting, firsthand accounts clearly labeled as such, and process analysis that starts with ordinary explanations before stronger hypotheses.

The archive is intentionally detailed because institutional asymmetry is detailed. A single form letter can close a complaint; testing whether that closure was adequate can require mapping the process, the records, the timelines, and the officials who controlled review. Detail still has to serve a defined evidentiary function: record, process, decision-maker, deadline, conflict screen, or test. Public-record claims are sourced to documentation. Firsthand observations and sealed-record-dependent claims are labeled as such.
