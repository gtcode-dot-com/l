## **University of Maryland Researchers Develop Robots for Complex Household Tasks [🔗](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/national-robotics-week-2026/#university-of-maryland)**

To bring robots into everyday life, researchers at the
[University of Maryland](https://www.umiacs.umd.edu/news-events/news/umd-researchers-advance-robotics-perform-complex-household-tasks)

are developing AI-powered humanoid systems capable of performing complex household tasks with greater autonomy.

The project centers on building robot foundation models that unify perception, planning and control. Using the
[NVIDIA Isaac](https://developer.nvidia.com/isaac?size=n_6_n&sort-field=featured&sort-direction=desc)

open robotics development platform, researchers can create photorealistic, high-fidelity virtual home environments populated with diverse objects and layouts, allowing robots to practice millions of task variations and safely test rare or complex scenarios.

[NVIDIA RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell GPUs](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/products/workstations/professional-desktop-gpus/rtx-pro-6000-family/)

for training large models and
[NVIDIA Jetson AGX Thor](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/autonomous-machines/embedded-systems/jetson-thor/)

developer kits for efficient deployment on physical robots help bridge the gap between research and real-world applications.

[![](https://blogs.nvidia.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Seungjae-Lee-UMIACS-1680x1122.jpg)](https://blogs.nvidia.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Seungjae-Lee-UMIACS-scaled.jpg)

By combining advancements in generative AI, sequential decision-making and scalable computing, the work represents a key step toward general-purpose robots that can support people in homes, healthcare settings and beyond.

## **Accelerating How Utility-Scale Solar Projects Are Built in the Field [🔗](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/national-robotics-week-2026/#maximo)**

[Maximo](https://maxrobotics.ai/)

, a solar robotics business incubated within The AES Corporation, recently completed a 100-megawatt solar installation using its robot fleet. Developed with NVIDIA accelerated computing,
[NVIDIA Omniverse libraries](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/omniverse/)

and the
[NVIDIA Isaac Sim framework](https://developer.nvidia.com/isaac/sim)

, Maximo demonstrated that autonomous installations can operate reliably for utility-scale projects.

The solution improves installation speed, safety and consistency, helping close the gap between rising demand for faster time to power and construction capacity.

![](https://blogs.nvidia.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Sunset_4Robots-960x540.jpg)

As solar expansion faces ongoing labor constraints and rising demand, AI-driven field robotics systems like Maximo are helping accelerate infrastructure buildout, reduce costs and redefine how energy projects are delivered.

## **Aigen Advances Sustainable Farming With Agricultural Robotics [🔗](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/national-robotics-week-2026/#aigen)**

To help regenerate the Earth,
[Aigen’s solar-powered autonomous robots](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nvidia-for-startups_aigen-regenerating-earth-with-robotics-and-activity-7444752936771170304-UMpl/)

are breaking farmers’ dependency on chemicals through precision weed control powered by vision AI.

The
[NVIDIA Inception](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/startups/)
startup is building a new kind of farming system that’s powered by clean energy and continuously enriched by data. Aigen’s fleet of solar-driven rovers uses advanced computer vision to identify and remove weeds, dramatically reducing the need for herbicides.

[![](https://blogs.nvidia.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Copy-of-Synthetic_Data.gif)](https://blogs.nvidia.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Copy-of-Synthetic_Data.gif)

Farming has no standard environment. Every field is different — different crops, different soil, different equipment, weeds, growth stages and geographies. That fragmentation makes real-world data collection slow, expensive and inconsistent. By post-training
[NVIDIA Cosmos open world foundation models](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/ai/cosmos/)

on their specialized data and harnessing
[NVIDIA Isaac Sim](https://developer.nvidia.com/isaac/sim)

pipelines, Aigen is building the system that generalizes for millions of agriculture scenarios.

On the ground, each rover runs inference using an
[NVIDIA Jetson Orin](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/autonomous-machines/embedded-systems/jetson-orin/)

edge AI module to distinguish crops from weeds in real time.

Using these rovers, farmers can grow crops more sustainably and profitably, using regenerative practices that heal the land and foster ecological balance.
