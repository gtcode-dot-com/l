# HPD Records Manipulation: Pre-Trial Evidence Tampering

**Author:** Paul Edward Lowndes
**Date of Report:** March 5, 2026
**Date of Incident:** Prior to December 2, 2022 (exact date to be confirmed)
**Location:** Honolulu Police Department, Records Division

---

## Summary

Prior to the trial before Judge Wilson M.N. Loo (December 2, 2022), I visited the HPD Records Division to obtain copies of my own police reports for use as evidence. The reports I sought corresponded to incidents I had personally filed, for which I possessed the original report numbers assigned at the time of filing, including Report No. 22-353421 and Report No. 22-365099.

## What Occurred

1. I presented my original police report numbers to the records clerk and requested copies.

2. The clerk stated that **those report numbers did not exist in the system.**

3. I showed the clerk photographic evidence of the original report numbers — photos I had taken at the time of filing.

4. The clerk asked me to wait. After a delay, she returned with printouts corresponding to **different report numbers** than those I had filed under.

5. The reports produced under these new numbers were **fully redacted.**

6. When I questioned the discrepancy, the clerk stated: **"Oh, they changed the report numbers."**

## Significance

Police report numbers are assigned at the time of filing and are not routinely reassigned. The replacement of original report numbers with new numbers — attached to fully redacted versions of the same reports — immediately before a court proceeding is consistent with deliberate records manipulation. This had the effect of:

- Depriving me of usable evidence for trial
- Creating a break in the documented chain between my original filings and the records HPD would produce if subpoenaed
- Obscuring the content of reports that documented a pattern of criminal harassment and HPD's own failure to act on those reports

## Evidentiary Context

This incident occurred in the same period as, and directly relates to, the proceedings before Judge Wilson Loo in which I allege judicial misconduct including witness coaching, suppression of objection, and subsequent sealing of the case record. The manipulation of HPD records prior to trial suggests institutional coordination predating the courtroom events.
